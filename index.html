About Me

I'm a sustainability professional with experience in international corporate engagement, data analysis, and education. I graduated from Ball State University in 2020, where I developed skills in environmental and economic modeling, sustainability reporting, and statistics. Serving as an environmental educator cemented my passion for nature, and I applied my skills to write impactful curriculum and optimize logistics for several non-profits.

I currently support global corporations with eliminating deforestation from their commodity supply chains by developing data tools and insights, offering 1:1 technical support and webinars, and convening with thought leaders on reporting standards and frameworks.

I am interested in the application of spatial data science in addressing deforestation, land use change, and other complex global issues. My current learning includes remote sensing of deforestation and land conversion, commodity supply chain data analysis and trceability, and statistical applications of Python and R to managing sustainability impacts in organizations. I am currently completing Google's Advanced Data Analytics Professional Certificate.
